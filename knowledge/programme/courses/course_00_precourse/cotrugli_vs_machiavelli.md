# Cotrugli vs Machiavelli: Framework Comparison
**Source:** Pre-course reading material

## Context

### Machiavelli's Prince (1513)
Written in fractured, violent Renaissance Italy — constant warfare, unstable alliances, assassinations. Not a commercial world. A zero-sum political battlefield.

**Core assumptions:** People are self-interested, fickle, fearful. Loyalty lasts only while incentives last.

**Central principles:**
- Power precedes morality
- Fear more reliable than love
- Ends justify means
- Appear virtuous; do not be virtuous
- Short-term decisiveness beats long-term goodwill

**Optimises for:** Retention of power, control, elimination of threats, short-term stability
**Game theory:** Single-shot, zero-sum games
**Works when:** High violence, low trust, no enforceable contracts, non-repeat interactions

### Cotrugli's Merchant (1458)
Written as a merchant in Mediterranean trade networks — repeat transactions across decades, credit-based commerce, reputation-dependent survival. An early network economy.

**Core assumptions:** Trade is repeated. Information travels through networks. Reputation precedes opportunity.

**Central principles:**
- Business is a craft
- Reputation is the primary asset
- Virtue is functional, not sentimental
- Long-term orientation dominates
- Mutual benefit sustains trade

**Optimises for:** Longevity, trust accumulation, network access, repeat business, intergenerational wealth
**Game theory:** Iterated, positive-sum games
**Works when:** Trade networks, repeat interactions, transparency through reputation, credit-based systems

## Direct Comparison

| Dimension | Machiavellian Prince | Cotruglian Merchant |
|---|---|---|
| Primary Domain | Politics & warfare | Commerce & trade |
| Game Type | Zero-sum, single-shot | Positive-sum, repeated |
| Core Asset | Power & fear | Trust & reputation |
| Ethics | Instrumental (optional) | Structural (necessary) |
| Time Horizon | Short-term survival | Long-term compounding |
| Strategy | Control, deception, dominance | Coordination, fairness, craft |
| Failure Mode | Revolt, coups, collapse | Network exclusion, slow decay |
| Modern Analogy | Autocrat / extractive CEO | Ecosystem builder / platform leader |

## The Critical Insight
Machiavelli is not "wrong." He is domain-specific. The problem arises when political survival logic is imported into commercial, networked, transparent systems. That misapplication creates structural failure.

## Why This Matters in the NEO Era
Modern economies now resemble Cotrugli's world: information travels instantly, reputation is global, transactions are repeated, networks amplify behaviour, trust collapses faster than ever.

Yet many institutions still run Machiavellian operating systems: short-term extraction, fear-based compliance, performative ethics, power concentration.

**Answer:** They are applying Prince logic in a Merchant world.

## One-Sentence Summary
Machiavelli teaches how to survive power in violent, zero-sum systems; Cotrugli teaches how to compound value in trust-based, networked systems — the tragedy of modern leadership is applying the first where only the second can work.
